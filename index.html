A Tribute Page To

Sir James Belu Iweobi KSM.

A simple Google search of the word “tribute” defines tribute as “an act, statement, or gift that is intended to show gratitude, respect, or admiration.” It goes on to give further synonyms as accolade, praise, commendation, acclaim, acclamation, salute. These words do not do justice to the man this webpage is about.

Blessed with the love of a wonder wife and mother to his children, Sir James B. Iweobi has been married to Mrs. Rosaline Iweobi for more than 35years which is blessed with 6 sons.

My life journey started with my entrance into this world and my first impression was those of my parents, it was their love I felt first. My father worked long and hard so that my brothers and I might have the opportunities he never had, a symbol of his love.

My father’s educational background would come as a surprise to many given his accomplishment in life, he has succeeded in 3 different business industries which require a sound technical knowledge of each industry from medicine to engineer, all on a standard 6 educational background, this is testament to his intelligence.

Sir James taught me to be a man, not just a human who has a penis, but a man who looks after his family, a man who can be trusted to lead when called upon, in my in 2nd year in university, I had a medical emergency that required a surgery to be done on me within 48hrs, a close friend in school had deposited a huge part of his school fees to have the hospital commence treatment on me, at the time technology wasn’t what we have today and when my father got there, he was able to refund the friend and even gave him a tip for taking care of me.

My dad’s favorite quote is “The reality of life is truth”, he has strived to live his life by those words, he ascended from a member of his community union to the president of the union, his unique style of handling issues is one of his many gifts I hope to emulate, although he is no more the president of the union, members of the union and other people still seek his opinion especially when it involves the community. The wisdom he exhumes, makes me very proud to have his blood cursing through my veins.

Also a very generous man, Sir James has given back so much to his community, my father once said to me “when God blesses you, a portion of that gift belongs to the people around you”, being a business man that he is, he used an example of money. He said, “When you’re blessed with N100, a percentage of that money belongs to your brothers and to the people around you”, he taught us at an early age to sharing and to give when we can. My father had this funny way of giving back to the church, the house in our home town is filled with things he donated during church thanks giving services and then bought back at the church’s auction and bazaar for more than twice the price he had bought them in the market.

Recognized by his church for his generosity and service to humanity, Sir James B. Iweobi was inducted into the order of Knighthood of Saint Mulumba (KSM) founded in Nigeria in 1953, it has 10,771 members, it is a great honor to his family to be counted as one of them.

It has been said that a son is the best assurance of immortality and it has also been said that a father wants to leave the best part of himself to someone else, his son. To be able to the son by hand when he is little; lead him out into the spring to show him the glories of God, the wonders of nature; to heart his confidences when he is older; to guide the way to go. To be able to point with pride and say, “see this boy? This is boy is my son!” this is passing on a part of himself, as one of his sons, I am happy and proud to have this responsibility on my shoulders.
